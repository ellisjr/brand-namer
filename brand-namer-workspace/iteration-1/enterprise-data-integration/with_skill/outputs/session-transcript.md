## STEP 01b: Territories & Whitespace

### Competitive Whitespace Mapping

**Competitor naming analysis:**

| Competitor | Name Type | Energy | Pattern |
|---|---|---|---|
| MuleSoft | Compound (animal + quality) | Metaphorical -- beast of burden | Animal + abstract |
| Boomi | Coined/Sound-symbolic | Playful, bouncy, approachable | Onomatopoeic feel |
| Fivetran | Blend (five + transform) | Technical, abbreviated | Number + truncation |
| Airbyte | Compound (air + byte) | Technical, digital-native | Element + tech unit |
| Workato | Blend (work + automato) | Action-oriented | Verb + suffix |
| Tray.io | Found word + domain hack | Simple, physical metaphor | Object + .io |

**Dominant patterns:** The integration space clusters around (a) technical compound/blends (Fivetran, Airbyte), (b) playful/approachable coined words (Boomi, Workato), and (c) physical metaphors (Tray, MuleSoft's mule). Phonetically, the set skews short (1-2 syllables for the core word) and bright/forward.

**Whitespace identified:**
1. **Institutional weight** -- nobody in this space sounds like Palantir or Snowflake. There's room for gravitas.
2. **Precision/measurement vocabulary** -- competitors describe transport (mule, tray, pipe), not the intelligence of routing and mapping.
3. **Architectural vocabulary** -- the "connective tissue" metaphor is unexploited. Structural terms from architecture, textiles, engineering.
4. **Classical/foreign-root** -- no competitor uses Latin or Greek. Room for elevated vocabulary.
5. **Orchestration/musical** -- the conductor metaphor (orchestrating many systems) is powerful and unoccupied.

### Naming Territories (5 defined)

**Territory 1: Precision Infrastructure**
- Emotional promise: The backbone you can trust. Engineered, exacting, load-bearing.
- Semantic field: bridge, beam, lattice, mesh, truss, spine, stratum, conduit, circuit, relay
- Phonetic profile: Hard consonants (k, t, d), crisp vowels (i, e), 1-2 syllables. Competence + Ruggedness.
- Anti-patterns: Soft/warm, playful, consumer-friendly

**Territory 2: Orchestration & Flow**
- Emotional promise: Everything in harmony. Complex systems, conducted with elegance.
- Semantic field: conductor, chorus, tempo, cadence, fugue, score, compose, harmony, sequence, baton
- Phonetic profile: Balanced -- flowing consonants (l, r, s) with rhythmic stress. Sophistication + Competence.
- Anti-patterns: Staccato, aggressive, industrial

**Territory 3: Cartographic Intelligence**
- Emotional promise: We see the whole map. Navigation, clarity, the elevated view.
- Semantic field: meridian, azimuth, transit, bearing, survey, contour, datum, waypoint, parallax, isoline
- Phonetic profile: Crisp, precise, Latin/Greek roots. Sage archetype energy.
- Anti-patterns: Warm/fuzzy, colloquial, informal

**Territory 4: Textile / Weaving**
- Emotional promise: Interwoven systems. The fabric that holds everything together.
- Semantic field: warp, weft, selvage, loom, weave, bobbin, shuttle, spool, thread, braid, plait, twill
- Phonetic profile: Mix of hard and soft -- W/L/TH for weaving, hard T/K for structure.
- Anti-patterns: Too literal (FabricConnect), too soft (Silken)

**Territory 5: Signal & Translation**
- Emotional promise: We speak every system's language. The universal translator.
- Semantic field: cipher, codec, parse, render, relay, signal, channel, trunk, switch, patch, port
- Phonetic profile: Short, technical, developer-credible. Hard consonants, monosyllabic.
- Anti-patterns: Marketing-speak, warm/approachable over technical

**Explored but dropped:**
- "Personified / Domain Figures" -- dropped because developer tools naming for middleware platforms benefits more from institutional/technical energy than warmth. The daily-use argument is weaker here -- data engineers interact with the platform but don't "talk to" it.
- "Nature/Animal" -- dropped. The MuleSoft "beast of burden" metaphor is already in the space; another animal risks looking derivative.
