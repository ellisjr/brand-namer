Based on the user's input, here is the working brief:

- **Naming target:** The company/platform (standalone brand)
- **What it does:** AI-native cybersecurity platform -- anomaly detection, automated incident response, continuous compliance monitoring
- **Primary audience:** CISOs and security teams at mid-to-large enterprises (500-10K employees)
- **Secondary audience:** Engineers who use the CLI daily; C-suite who see it on board decks
- **Personality:** Authoritative, technically credible, modern, serious but not gimmicky
- **Brand archetype:** Sage + Ruler (intelligence + institutional authority)
- **Feeling spectrum:**
  - Authority vs Approachability: 7/10 (leans authoritative)
  - Innovation vs Heritage: 8/10 (strongly innovation-forward)
  - Precision vs Playfulness: 8/10 (strongly precise)
  - Intimacy vs Scale: 7/10 (leans toward scale/enterprise)
- **Name's #1 job:** Signal credibility -- engineers trust it, C-suite approves it, analysts write about it
- **Naming architecture:** Standalone brand
- **Competitors:** CrowdStrike, SentinelOne, Darktrace, Wiz, Orca Security, Palo Alto Networks
- **Constraints:** Must work as CLI command (short, typeable, no spaces), must work in Gartner headline, must not sound like a competitor
- **Seed words:** (inferred from brief) anomaly, detect, signal, compliance, response, vigilance, sentinel, watch, observe

**Vertical Code Calibration -- Cybersecurity (two patterns identified):**

1. **Institutional pattern:** Military/intelligence vocabulary, compound names, institutional weight. Signals "we protect the Fortune 500." Examples: CrowdStrike, SentinelOne, Palo Alto Networks, Fortinet
2. **Minimal-technical pattern:** Short, clever, developer-credible. Signals "built by engineers, not marketed by suits." Examples: Wiz, Snyk, Vanta, Drata, Orca

The brief calls for BOTH: engineer-respected (pattern 2) AND C-suite comfortable (pattern 1). The sweet spot is names like Stripe, Datadog, Snowflake -- technically credible without being exclusionary.

**Trust-Without-Claims Lexicon applied:** Avoid "secure," "shield," "protect," "defend," "safe," "guard" (over-claiming words that trigger skepticism in enterprise buyers). **Governance/Audit Vocabulary noted for mining:** attest, ledger, trail, evidence, record, docket, gate, check, threshold, standard, mark, certify, lineage, trace, provenance, assess, gauge, score, grade, signal, flag.

**Developer / Technical Credibility Signaling:** Must be CLI-friendly (short, typeable, lowercase-compatible). Favor found words, precise vocabulary, 1-2 syllables for CLI. Avoid -ify/-ly/-hub suffixes.

**Platform vs Point-Solution:** This is a PLATFORM (anomaly detection + incident response + compliance = multi-function). Name must NOT describe a single function. Abstract/evocative preferred.

**Pattern analysis:** The cybersecurity space clusters heavily around:
- Military/combat metaphors (Strike, Sentinel, Armor, Shield, Guard)
- Dark/stealth vocabulary (Dark, Shadow, Stealth, Phantom)
- Compound names with aggressive energy (CrowdStrike, SentinelOne, Darktrace)
- The newer wave uses short, clever, dev-credible names (Wiz, Snyk, Vanta, Drata)

**Whitespace identified:**
- **Precision/measurement vocabulary** -- nobody in cybersecurity is using watchmaking, surveying, or assaying metaphors
- **Institutional calm** -- the space is all aggression and vigilance. A name that signals quiet competence rather than aggressive defense would stand apart
- **Governance/compliance-forward** -- most names signal "protection." Few signal "continuous governance" which is this platform's differentiator
- **Infrastructure/foundational** -- naming the platform as bedrock rather than weapon

**Territory exploration (Tree of Thought -- 10 territories explored, 5 selected):**

**Explored and DROPPED:**
- "Dark Intelligence" (stealth/shadow vocabulary) -- too close to Darktrace, overlaps with existing patterns
- "Predator/Hunter" (active threat-hunting animal metaphors) -- too close to CrowdStrike/Orca aggressive energy
- "Fortress/Stronghold" (defensive architecture) -- over-claiming, triggers Trust-Without-Claims filter
- "Neural/Synaptic" (AI-forward naming) -- too "bolted-on AI" sounding, pharma-adjacent
- "Command Center" (military operations) -- too close to existing institutional compound pattern

**SELECTED TERRITORIES:**

**Territory 1: Precision Instrument**
- Emotional promise: "We measure with exactitude what others miss"
- Semantic field: caliber, assay, gauge, scope, lens, parallax, azimuth, sounding, benchmark, baseline
- Phonetic profile: Crisp consonants (k, t, s), high/front vowels (i, e), 1-2 syllables. Competence dimension.
- Anti-patterns: Should NOT sound like a lab instrument brand or feel overly clinical

**Territory 2: Quiet Authority**
- Emotional promise: "We see everything. We say little. We act decisively."
- Semantic field: attest, certify, warrant, resolve, accord, decree, precept, canon, rubric, verdict
- Phonetic profile: Weighty consonants (d, g, b), balanced stress, institutional rhythm. Ruler archetype.
- Anti-patterns: Should NOT sound like a law firm or government agency

**Territory 3: Signal & Visibility**
- Emotional promise: "We surface what matters from the noise"
- Semantic field: signal, beacon, flare, trace, detect, reveal, illuminate, clarity, aperture, spectrum
- Phonetic profile: Fricatives (s, f) for flow, mixed vowels, moderate length. Sage archetype.
- Anti-patterns: Should NOT sound like a marketing analytics tool

**Territory 4: Foundational Infrastructure**
- Emotional promise: "We are the bedrock your security posture is built on"
- Semantic field: stratum, basalt, bedrock, keystone, lintel, core, root, ground, base, anchor
- Phonetic profile: Hard consonants (k, g, d), back vowels (o, u), short syllables. Ruggedness + Competence.
- Anti-patterns: Should NOT sound like a hosting/cloud infrastructure company

**Territory 5: Governance & Attestation**
- Emotional promise: "Continuous proof. Unbroken chain of evidence."
- Semantic field: attest, ledger, docket, lineage, provenance, chain, record, audit, witness, certify
- Phonetic profile: Clean, institutional, Latin-adjacent. Sage + Ruler.
- Anti-patterns: Should NOT sound like an accounting/GRC-only tool
