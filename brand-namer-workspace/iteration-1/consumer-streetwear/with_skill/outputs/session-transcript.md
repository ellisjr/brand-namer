### Step 01b — Territories & Whitespace

**Reference loaded:** `references/brand-psychology.md`

#### Competitive Whitespace Mapping

Your competitors cluster into distinct naming patterns:

| Competitor | Naming pattern | Energy |
|---|---|---|
| **Pangaia** | Coined/foreign (pan + Gaia) | Science-nature, utopian, clean |
| **Story MFG** | Descriptive compound | Craft-narrative, artisanal, warm |
| **69 Worldwide** | Confrontational number + descriptor | Sexual, provocative, global |
| **Brain Dead** | Absurdist compound | Culture-graphic, punk, chaotic |
| **Stussy** | Surname/personified | Classic, surf-skate heritage, founder-coded |

**Pattern:** The space splits between earnest-craft (Story MFG, Pangaia) and aggressive-absurdist (Brain Dead, 69). Stussy occupies the heritage middle.

**Whitespace:** Nobody occupies the **dark-conscious** space — the intersection of environmental awareness and urban grit. Pangaia is too clean/bright. Story MFG is too hippie. Brain Dead is too chaotic (no sustainability angle). The whitespace is: **names that feel dark, urban, and slightly dangerous while carrying undercurrents of consciousness and material honesty.**

Also available: **material/process vocabulary** (the making of clothes) as a naming territory nobody in this competitive set is using. And **East London/urban environment** vocabulary.

#### Naming Territories (5 defined)

**Territory 1: Dark Material**
- Emotional promise: Raw authenticity, materiality, honest about what things are made of
- Semantic field: textile process words (weft, nap, selvage, bolt, drape), material states (raw, crude, char, soot, oxide), deadstock/reclaimed vocabulary
- Phonetic profile: Hard consonants, short syllables, Anglo-Saxon roots. Plosives for impact.
- Anti-patterns: Should NOT sound precious, artisanal-twee, or "craft fair"

**Territory 2: Urban Nocturne**
- Emotional promise: Night city energy, East London after dark, street-lit edges
- Semantic field: urban/night vocabulary (dusk, murk, soot, grime, flint, tar, neon, kerb, alley), London-specific (yard, manor, ends, estate)
- Phonetic profile: Dark vowels (uh, oh), hard stops, monosyllabic preference
- Anti-patterns: Should NOT sound gentrified, tourist-London, or Dickensian

**Territory 3: Rebel Consciousness**
- Emotional promise: Anti-corporate, aware but not preachy, punk meets planet
- Semantic field: defiance (revolt, refuse, defy, spite, scrap), consciousness (wake, aware, lucid, sober), tension words (friction, static, clash)
- Phonetic profile: Sharp fricatives and plosives, staccato rhythm
- Anti-patterns: Should NOT sound like an NGO, a B-corp tagline, or a wellness brand

**Territory 4: Animal / Nature Curator** (default territory per skill rules for consumer/lifestyle)
- Emotional promise: Creatures that embody the brand's action — scavenging, recycling, nocturnal urban survival
- Semantic field: urban wildlife (crow, moth, fox, rat, magpie, pigeon, kestrel), scavenger/survivor creatures, dark-natured plants (briar, sloe, nettle, dock, hemlock)
- Phonetic profile: Short, concrete, imageable
- Anti-patterns: Should NOT be cute, zoo-like, or nature-documentary

**Territory 5: Arbitrary / Absurd Collision**
- Emotional promise: Zero logical connection to sustainability or streetwear — pure distinctiveness through cognitive dissonance
- Semantic field: Random concrete nouns with strong phonetics and visual imagery, tested on sound/feel only
- Phonetic profile: 1-2 syllables, hard consonants, vivid mental image
- Anti-patterns: Should NOT be explained or rationalized

**Territories explored and dropped:**
- "Heritage Craft" — too close to Story MFG's positioning
- "Utopian Future" — too close to Pangaia
- "Streetwear Code/Alphanumeric" — too close to A-COLD-WALL, Palace
- "Personified/Founder" — user didn't signal interest in personal naming

**Session mode:** Explorer (detailed brief with clear taste signals = show breadth first)
