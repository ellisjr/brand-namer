## STEP 01a: BRAND BRIEF INTAKE

### Brief Summary (from user input)

**Naming target:** A small-batch artisanal hot sauce brand
**What it does:** Produces fermented hot sauces (6+ month fermentation) using locally-sourced peppers from Willamette Valley farms. Range from mild smoky chipotle to Carolina Reaper.
**Primary audience:** Food enthusiasts aged 25-45 who shop at farmers' markets, specialty grocery stores, and follow food creators on TikTok/Instagram
**Brand personality:** Craft, authentic, bold, earthy, with a bit of irreverence
**Archetype:** Creator primary (craft, making, intentionality) with Outlaw secondary (irreverence, boldness, rule-breaking)
**Prototype target:** Fly By Jing energy — cultural, distinctive, personal, unapologetic
**Anti-targets:** Not another "fire" or "burn" name. Not Frank's RedHot energy. Not generic hot sauce bro-culture.
**Competitors:** Yellowbird, Secret Aardvark, Heatonist, Fly By Jing, Cholula, Truff
**Constraints:** Must work on a bottle label AND as an Instagram handle
**Naming architecture:** Standalone brand
**Name's #1 job:** Be distinctive and shareable — something that makes someone pick up the bottle and ask "what IS this?"
**Seed words (inferred from brief):** ferment, earth, bold, craft, valley, pepper, slow, wild, irreverent

### Feeling Spectrum
- Authority vs Approachability: 4 (lean approachable)
- Innovation vs Heritage: 5 (balanced — modern craft)
- Precision vs Playfulness: 6 (lean playful/irreverent)
- Intimacy vs Scale: 3 (intimate, small-batch)
- Simplicity vs Complexity: 4 (simple but layered)

### Emotional Temperature Mapping
- Thermal: Warm to hot (fire, ferment, earth)
- Weight: Medium-heavy (grounded, earthy, substantial)
- Energy: Medium-high (bold but not frantic)
- Texture: Rough/gritty (raw, craft, authentic)
- Brightness: Medium-dark (earthy, smoky, not neon)

### Price-Signal Calibration
Target register: **Masstige** — elevated but approachable. Think Fly By Jing pricing ($10-15/bottle), specialty grocery positioning, Instagram-ready packaging. Not ultra-luxury (Truff territory) but clearly above commodity (Cholula).

## STEP 01b: TERRITORIES & WHITESPACE

### Competitive Whitespace Mapping

**Competitor naming patterns:**
| Competitor | Naming Pattern | Category |
|---|---|---|
| Yellowbird | Color + Animal (arbitrary/compound) | Compound |
| Secret Aardvark | Adjective + Animal (arbitrary/absurd) | Compound |
| Heatonist | Heat + -ist suffix (coined) | Coined |
| Fly By Jing | Phrase + Name (cultural/personal) | Evocative |
| Cholula | Place name (Mexican city) | Foreign-root |
| Truff | Truncated "truffle" (ingredient-inspired) | Found word/blend |

**Dominant patterns:**
- Animal names are well-represented (Yellowbird, Secret Aardvark)
- Heat/spice vocabulary is the obvious default (Heatonist, countless others)
- Ingredient nods are common (Truff)
- Cultural/personal names are rare — Fly By Jing stands alone in this territory

**Whitespace identified:**
1. **Process/craft vocabulary** — nobody is naming from the making process itself (fermentation, culturing, aging)
2. **Earth/terroir vocabulary** — the Willamette Valley connection, the soil, the land
3. **Sensory dissonance** — using unexpected sensory words (cold for hot, quiet for loud flavors)
4. **Arbitrary/absurd with irreverent energy** — the Cowshed/Liquid Death play adapted for hot sauce

### Naming Territories (4 defined)

**Territory 1: "Slow Burn Craft"**
- Emotional promise: Patience, intentionality, the beauty of time-transformed ingredients
- Semantic field: ferment, culture, mother, cure, age, steep, bloom, brine, batch, still
- Phonetic profile: Grounded, warm consonants (m, n, l), medium weight, earthy vowels
- Anti-patterns: Should NOT sound rushed, industrial, or mass-market. No "quick" or "instant" energy.

**Territory 2: "Valley Earth"**
- Emotional promise: Terroir, place, the specific land these peppers grow in
- Semantic field: loam, clay, basalt, valley, furrow, root, soil, silt, alluvium, ridge
- Phonetic profile: Heavy, grounded, Anglo-Saxon roots, single syllables, dark vowels
- Anti-patterns: Should NOT sound precious, pastoral, or farmer-chic. No "homestead" energy.

**Territory 3: "Animal/Nature Curator"** (default territory for consumer brands)
- Emotional promise: Wild, instinctive, creature energy that embodies boldness or patience or sting
- Semantic field: creatures that ferment/forage/sting/persist — wasps, crows, foxes, badgers, beetles, salamanders, newts
- Phonetic profile: Concrete, imageable, 1-2 syllables, strong consonants for bold energy
- Anti-patterns: Should NOT duplicate the aardvark/bird animal-hot-sauce pattern. No cute animals.

**Territory 4: "Irreverent Dissonance"**
- Emotional promise: Confidence, swagger, the unexpected — a name that makes you do a double-take
- Semantic field: arbitrary/absurd associations, sensory inversions, cultural tension words
- Phonetic profile: Punchy, short, surprising, possibly uncomfortable — the Liquid Death play
- Anti-patterns: Should NOT be shock-for-shock's-sake. No profanity. No "trying too hard."

**Territories explored and dropped:**
- "Heat Mastery" — too close to competitor territory (Heatonist). Every hot sauce brand lives here.
- "Cultural Origin" — the Cholula/Fly By Jing play. Without a genuine cultural narrative, this feels appropriative.
