## Key Observations

1. **Geological/material vocabulary was the richest vein** for this brief. Basalt, Flint, Obsidian, Slate, Ore, Quarry all emerged as strong candidates from geological mining.

2. **Spirits/fragrance vocabulary borrowed well** into men's skincare. Proof, Neat, Vetiver, Tonic, Dram, Cask -- the spirits register maps naturally to "elevated masculine" positioning.

3. **Philosophical/virtue names had unexpectedly strong fit.** Stoic, Tenet, Merit, Vigil -- the "skincare as masculine discipline" angle is distinctive whitespace that no competitor occupies.

4. **Compounds were less productive** for this brief (as expected for consumer/beauty). The strongest candidates were overwhelmingly found words, foreign roots, and sensory names.

5. **The matte-black-bottle test was a powerful filter.** Names that "look good on a matte black bottle" naturally selected for short, hard, consonant-heavy words with visual weight.

6. **Japanese vocabulary (Sumi, Raku, Kuro) provided an Aesop-adjacent channel** that felt authentic rather than appropriative, given the brief's explicit reference to Aesop as a prototype target.
