# Brand Brief: Premium Men's Skincare Line

## Naming Target
A premium men's skincare brand — standalone brand identity for a product line including cleanser, moisturizer, eye cream, and SPF.

## Audience
Men aged 28-45 who are entering the skincare category. Not the "Axe Body Spray" demographic — these are men who read GQ, own a nice watch, appreciate quality, and would spend $40-60 on a moisturizer. They would never walk into Sephora. They want skincare that respects their intelligence and masculinity.

## Brand Personality
- **Elevated but masculine** — premium without veering feminine
- **Clinical-meets-luxury** — the precision of a dermatologist's recommendation with the packaging of a luxury brand
- **No-nonsense** — confident, direct, doesn't over-explain
- **Confident** — knows what it is, doesn't try too hard

## Archetypes
Primary: **Ruler** (control, prestige, commanding) crossed with **Sage** (intelligence, expertise, precision).
Secondary: **Creator** (crafted, intentional, design-forward).

## Feeling Spectrum
- Authority over Approachability (7/10) — respected first, liked second
- Innovation balanced with Heritage (5/10) — not retro, not futuristic
- Precision over Playfulness (8/10) — no-nonsense
- Intimacy-leaning (4/10) — personal ritual, not mass market
- Moderate Complexity (6/10) — suggests expertise without being clinical

## Prototype Targets
"Aesop meets The Ordinary but specifically for men." Aesop brings the apothecary sophistication and literary-intellectual energy. The Ordinary brings clinical transparency and no-bullshit directness. The intersection: intelligent luxury skincare that respects the consumer.

Other brand energy benchmarks: Tom Ford (masculine luxury), Byredo (understated Scandinavian premium), Le Labo (artisanal precision).

## Competitive Landscape
| Competitor | Naming Style | Energy |
|---|---|---|
| Lumin | Latin-derived, clinical-light | Moderately premium, slightly generic |
| Kiehl's | Founder surname | Heritage apothecary |
| Bulldog | Animal mascot, friendly | Accessible, playful — too casual |
| Harry's | Founder-esque first name | Approachable everyman |
| Hims | Pronoun-based, casual | DTC millennial, too casual/cute |

## Constraints & Anti-Patterns
- NOT feminine (no Glossier/Glow Recipe energy)
- NOT aggressive (no War Paint/Tough Mudder energy)
- NOT too clinical (no CeraVe/Cetaphil energy)
- NOT playful/cute (no Hims/Bulldog energy)
- Must work on matte black packaging
- Must signal Nordstrom/Saks-level quality
- Must work as DTC brand name

## Name's #1 Job
Signal that this is elevated, masculine skincare — the name alone should tell a man "this is for you" without saying "FOR MEN" anywhere on the bottle.

## Seed Words
matte, clinical, precision, elevated, ritual, discipline, refined, essential
