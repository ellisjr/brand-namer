# Brand Brief: AI Legal Document Review Platform

## Naming Target
An AI-powered legal document review platform. Standalone brand name for the company/product.

## What It Does
Uses LLMs to analyze contracts, flag risks, extract key terms, and compare against playbooks. Core value prop: AI-powered precision for contract analysis at enterprise scale.

## Primary Audience
Legal operations teams and in-house counsel at companies with 10+ lawyers. These are sophisticated buyers who evaluate enterprise software through procurement committees. They need to trust the tool with high-stakes legal work.

## Brand Personality
Authoritative and precise, but NOT stuffy. The energy of Clio and Relativity — modern, clean, respected by legal professionals without feeling like a law firm or a Latin textbook. Think: the kind of name a General Counsel would put in a board presentation without hesitation, and a legal ops associate would type into Slack 30 times a day without eye-rolling.

## Key Constraints
1. **No Latin cliches:** Lexicon, Juris, Veritas, and the entire Lex-/Jur- naming pattern are exhausted in legaltech. The brief explicitly excludes them.
2. **Regulated terms:** Cannot use "attorney," "counsel," "legal advice," or "law" — these imply bar-licensed practice.
3. **International from day one:** UK and EU markets. Name must work across English, and not create problems in major European languages.
4. **Platform, not point solution:** Must support expansion beyond document review. The name shouldn't describe a single function.
5. **Signal 'built for lawyers'** through register and authority, not through legal vocabulary.

## Competitive Landscape
- **Kira Systems** — compound, named after founder's daughter, warm/human
- **eBrevia** — Latin-root coined (brevis = brief/short), feels dated
- **Luminance** — physics/light metaphor, evocative, sophisticated
- **Ironclad** — found word, strength metaphor, decisive
- **ContractPodAi** — descriptive compound, very literal
- **Harvey** — personified/human name, approachable, AI-forward

## Prototype Energy
Clio (mythological, single word, authority through unexpected vocabulary) and Relativity (physics metaphor, institutional weight, intellectual). The user wants names that earn authority through phonetic weight and unexpected vocabulary rather than Latin etymology.
