## Step 01a: Brand Brief Intake

### Naming Target
Sustainable streetwear brand based in East London. Limited-run drops of hoodies, tees, and accessories using deadstock fabrics and recycled materials.

### Audience
18-30 year olds who care about sustainability but don't want to look like they shop at a hemp store.

### Brand Personality
Rebellious, conscious, urban, dark, anti-corporate, craft-driven.

### Archetype
Primary: Outlaw (liberation, rebellion). Secondary: Creator (making, design, craft).

### Competitors
Pangaia, Story MFG, 69 Worldwide, Brain Dead, Stussy

### Vibe Benchmark
"Intersection of Stussy and Patagonia"

### Name's #1 Job
Look credible on a tag -- signal streetwear authenticity first; sustainability discovered, not announced.

### Constraints
- One word or short (2-3 syllables max)
- Must work as fashion label (tag, embroidery)
- Streetwear register, not eco-brand
- No B2B compounds (-works, -craft, -wise)

### Phonetic Target
Hard consonants, short syllables, plosives (k, d, g, t), dark vowels. Outlaw energy.

### Feeling Spectrum
- Authority vs Approachability: 4 (edgy but not intimidating)
- Innovation vs Heritage: 6 (forward but grounded)
- Precision vs Playfulness: 4 (deliberate, not silly)
- Intimacy vs Scale: 3 (cult label feel)
- Simplicity vs Complexity: 3 (streetwear = clean, punchy)

## Step 01b: Territories & Whitespace

### Tree of Thought: Territory Exploration
10 candidate territories generated, evaluated on differentiation/alignment/generative potential, pruned to 5.

### Final Territories

1. **Dark Salvage** -- Beauty in what was discarded. Reclamation as rebellion.
   - Semantic: salvage, scrap, remnant, relic, offcut, surplus, deadstock
   - Phonetic: hard consonants, dark vowels, industrial weight
   - Anti-patterns: nothing clean, aspirational, or eco-optimistic

2. **Night Borough** -- The nocturnal city. London after dark, warehouse culture.
   - Semantic: soot, grime, kerb, alley, smog, dusk, vault, fog
   - Phonetic: short Anglo-Saxon words, urban texture
   - Anti-patterns: nothing pastoral, sunny, suburban

3. **Outlaw Ecology** -- Punk environmentalism. Nature reclaiming, feral not tame.
   - Semantic: feral, weed, rot, rust, moss, spore, blight, lichen, thorn
   - Phonetic: tension between natural and industrial
   - Anti-patterns: nothing Patagonia-clean or Story MFG-hippie

4. **Urban Fauna** -- City creatures that thrive in the margins. Scavengers, adapters, survivors.
   - Semantic: crow, moth, rat, fox, pigeon, magpie, kestrel, vulture
   - Phonetic: short, punchy animal names, hard sounds
   - Anti-patterns: nothing cute, cuddly, or safari-adjacent

5. **Material Rebellion** -- Physical act of making as protest. Fabric, tools, process.
   - Semantic: warp, weft, bolt, dye, cut, sew, selvage, raw, stitch
   - Phonetic: monosyllabic craft words, tactile consonants
   - Anti-patterns: nothing precious, artisanal-cute, or Etsy-adjacent

### Competitive Whitespace
Competitors split into: clean/aspirational (Pangaia), provocative/absurdist (69 Worldwide, Brain Dead), craft/hippie (Story MFG), heritage/founder (Stussy). **Whitespace:** dark environmentalism -- sustainability that sounds like it came from a warehouse squat, not a lab or commune.

## Key Findings

1. **Strongest conceptual find:** REFUSE -- the only name in the pool that literally means both "to reject" (the brand's attitude) and "waste material" (the brand's medium). Maximum memetic potential.

2. **Strongest metaphorical find:** CROW/MOTH -- urban scavengers with layered meanings. Crow collects (deadstock sourcing), is black (brand aesthetic), and thrives in cities. Moth eats fabric (textile connection), is nocturnal (dark aesthetic), and transforms (caterpillar to moth).

3. **Competitive whitespace validated:** No competitor occupies "dark environmentalism." The territory between Stussy's cultural edge and Patagonia's environmental credentials is genuinely open.

4. **Trend-cycle resilience:** Monosyllabic found words (Scrap, Crow, Moth, Murk, Soot, Char) are structurally timeless. Japanese borrowings (Kizu, Kuro) carry higher trend risk.

5. **Fashion conflict flags:** Relaxed per SKILL.md guidance but not skipped. 3 confirmed fashion-industry conflicts (Void, Savage, Refused), 14 uncertain flags requiring validation. Zero flags would be a failure on a 216-name pool -- the 25 flags demonstrate appropriate diligence.
