#### Definitions for Key Picks

**Vernier** (n.) — A small auxiliary scale allowing fine measurement between the main scale divisions. Named after Pierre Vernier (1580-1637), French mathematician.
As a brand: "The layer of precision between your systems."

**Datum** (n.) — A fixed starting point for measurement; a reference from which all other measurements derive. Plural: data.
As a brand: "The reference point for all your data connections."

**Weft** (n.) — The horizontal threads woven through the warp on a loom; the cross-threads that create fabric.
As a brand: "The threads that weave your systems into a single fabric."

**Vectis** (n.) — Latin: a lever or bar used for moving heavy objects. From vehere (to carry).
As a brand: "The lever that moves data between enterprise systems."

**Tessera** (n.) — Latin: a small tile in a mosaic; also a token of identity or admission.
As a brand: "Each system is a tile — we complete the mosaic."

**Nexora** (coined) — From Latin nex-/nect- (to bind, connect) + -ora (suffix).
As a brand: "The binding intelligence between your systems."

**Integris** (coined) — From Latin integer (whole, complete) + -is (suffix).
As a brand: "Making your tech stack whole."

**Assay** (n./v.) — To test the quality of a metal or ore; to evaluate or examine. From Old French "essai" (trial).
As a brand: "Every connection undergoes the assay — tested and verified."

**Fugue** (n.) — A musical composition where multiple voices enter successively, each imitating the theme. From Latin fuga (flight).
As a brand: "Multiple systems, one orchestrated composition."
