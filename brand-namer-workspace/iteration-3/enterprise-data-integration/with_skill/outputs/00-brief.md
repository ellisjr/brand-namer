# Brand Brief: Data Integration Middleware Platform

## What We're Naming
The company/platform itself — a data integration middleware that connects enterprise systems (Salesforce, Snowflake, Databricks, SAP) with a no-code visual builder and AI-powered data mapping.

## Audience
- **Primary:** Data engineering teams (daily users, technical, will judge on the name)
- **Secondary:** IT leaders / CIOs at enterprises running 50+ SaaS tools (budget holders, need enterprise trust signals)

## Core Value Proposition
Interoperability — "the connective tissue" between enterprise systems. Not a point solution for one integration; a platform for all of them.

## Brand Personality Target
- Technical but accessible (Stripe energy, not Accenture energy)
- Reliable, enterprise-grade (not scrappy/startup-feeling)
- The name should make a senior data engineer nod, not cringe

## Competitive Landscape
| Competitor | Name Analysis |
|---|---|
| MuleSoft | Animal metaphor (mule = beast of burden). Compound. Acquired by Salesforce. |
| Boomi | Coined/invented. Short. Playful feel. Originally Dell product. |
| Fivetran | Compound: Five + Tran(saction/sform). Technical feel. |
| Airbyte | Compound: Air + Byte. Technical + lightweight feel. |
| Workato | Blend: Work + auto. Action-oriented. |
| Tray.io | Found word (tray) + TLD. Simple, object-based. |

## Competitive Whitespace
Competitors cluster around technical compounds and coined/playful names. The authoritative-but-accessible zone (Stripe/Palantir energy) is wide open. Unoccupied territories: precision/instrument vocabulary, connective fabric metaphors, orchestration/music vocabulary, navigation/routing, classical institutional register.

## Naming Territories

### 1. Precision Instrument
- **Emotional promise:** Exactness, engineering excellence, calibrated reliability
- **Semantic field:** gauge, caliber, true, mark, point, grade, scope, lens, index, measure
- **Phonetic profile:** Clean consonants, high/front vowels (i, e), crisp endings, 1-2 syllables
- **Anti-patterns:** Soft/warm, playful, consumer-feeling

### 2. Connective Fabric
- **Emotional promise:** Everything works together seamlessly — the invisible layer that makes the stack whole
- **Semantic field:** mesh, weave, fabric, splice, bond, lattice, thread, loom, braid, filament
- **Phonetic profile:** Flowing consonants (l, m, s), balanced stress, material/textile feel
- **Anti-patterns:** Heavy/industrial, aggressive, clinical

### 3. Signal & Routing
- **Emotional promise:** Data finds its way — intelligent direction, clear pathways
- **Semantic field:** relay, conduit, channel, trunk, circuit, vector, bearing, signal, pulse, route
- **Phonetic profile:** Directional energy, plosives for motion (t, k), 1-2 syllables
- **Anti-patterns:** Static/passive, vague, abstract

### 4. Orchestration & Command
- **Emotional promise:** Mastery over complexity — conducting many instruments into one coherent output
- **Semantic field:** conductor, compose, chorus, baton, score, tempo, cadence, helm, steer, govern
- **Phonetic profile:** Institutional weight, rounded vowels, moderate length (2 syllables)
- **Anti-patterns:** Playful/light, single-function feeling, whimsical

### 5. Structural Foundation
- **Emotional promise:** The bedrock your data stack is built on — permanent, load-bearing, essential
- **Semantic field:** base, core, anchor, bedrock, keystone, truss, span, girder, pier, vault
- **Phonetic profile:** Hard consonants (k, d, g), back vowels, one-syllable Anglo-Saxon weight
- **Anti-patterns:** Ethereal, abstract, overly soft

## Integration Grammar Requirements
- "Powered by [Name]" — must carry authority
- "[Name] for Salesforce" — must feel peer-level
- "[Name] Connect" / "[Name] API" — must extend naturally

## Archetype
Sage/Ruler blend — competence is the dominant dimension. The brand knows things (Sage: truth, insight) and controls complexity (Ruler: authority, order). Not playful (Jester), not rebellious (Outlaw), not warm (Caregiver).

## Phonetic Direction
- Competence phonetics: high/front vowels (i, e), clean consonants, balanced stress
- 1-2 syllables preferred, 3 max
- Trochaic stress (STRONG-weak) most natural
- CLI-friendly: easy to type, no special characters
