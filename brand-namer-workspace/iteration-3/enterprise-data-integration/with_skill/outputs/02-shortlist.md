## Definitions for Non-Obvious Picks

**Assay** (n./v.) — "to test the quality of a metal or ore; to evaluate or examine"
  Etymology: Old French "essai" (trial, attempt), from Latin "exagium" (weighing)
  As a brand: "Every integration undergoes the assay — a test of quality and accuracy"

**Vectis** (n.) — Latin: a lever, bar, or crowbar; also an island (Isle of Wight)
  Etymology: Latin "vehere" (to carry, convey) — same root as "vector"
  As a brand: "The lever that moves data between systems"

**Pontis** (n.) — Latin genitive: "of the bridge" (from "pons, pontis")
  Etymology: Same root as "pontiff" (originally "bridge-builder")
  As a brand: "The bridge between your enterprise systems"

**Tessera** (n.) — Latin/Greek: a small tile used in mosaics; also a token or ticket
  Etymology: Greek "tessares" (four) — the square tiles in Roman mosaics
  As a brand: "Every system is a tile; we assemble the mosaic"

**Essai** (n.) — French: "test, trial, attempt" (also the origin of English "essay")
  Etymology: Old French "essai" from Latin "exagium" (weighing)
  As a brand: "The AI-native test of data quality" — contains "ai" naturally
