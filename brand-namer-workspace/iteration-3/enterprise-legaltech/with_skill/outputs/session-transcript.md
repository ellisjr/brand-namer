**Crowded zones:** Latin roots, light/clarity metaphors, protection/strength metaphors, descriptive tech compounds, generic AI suffixes

**Whitespace identified:**
1. Precision/measurement vocabulary -- no competitor occupies the exactitude register
2. Cartographic/navigational language -- unexplored in legaltech
3. Non-Latin scholarly authority -- only Relativity has claimed this adjacent space
4. Architectural/structural vocabulary -- untouched by competitors

### Territories Defined (5)

**1. Precision Instrument**
- Emotional promise: Calibrated, exacting, engineered for accuracy
- Semantic field: caliber, gauge, assay, benchmark, meridian, index, proof, standard, measure
- Phonetic profile: Hard consonants (k, t, d, b), crisp vowels (i, e), 2 syllables, trochaic stress
- Anti-patterns: No soft/warm sounds, no approximation, no "smart/AI" marketing

**2. Cartography / Navigator**
- Emotional promise: Navigate complex terrain with confidence
- Semantic field: bearing, traverse, chart, azimuth, transit, course, survey, waypoint, contour, datum
- Phonetic profile: Directional energy, motion verbs, clean 1-2 syllable words
- Anti-patterns: No adventure vocabulary (too casual), no compass/north (overused)

**3. Scholarly Authority**
- Emotional promise: Intellectual depth from science/academia (non-Latin)
- Semantic field: thesis, axiom, canon, theorem, postulate, premise, quorum, rubric, tenet, precept
- Phonetic profile: 3 syllables max if familiar, institutional weight, consonant or stable vowel endings
- Anti-patterns: No Latin cliches (veritas, juris), no pretentious obscurity

**4. Personified / Domain Figures**
- Emotional promise: Trusted human presence from history, mythology, or literature
- Semantic field: Historical jurists, literary legal figures, mythological wisdom figures
- Phonetic profile: 2-3 syllables, natural stress, cross-cultural
- Anti-patterns: No famous fictional lawyers (Harvey taken), no gendered assumptions

**5. Architecture / Framework**
- Emotional promise: Structural integrity and systematic thinking
- Semantic field: plinth, keystone, lintel, truss, cantilever, arbor, buttress, scaffold
- Phonetic profile: Strong consonant onsets, solid endings, compound potential
- Anti-patterns: No construction cliches (build, foundation overused)
