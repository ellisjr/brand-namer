# Brand Brief: Sustainable Streetwear Label

## What We're Naming
A standalone sustainable streetwear brand based in East London. Makes limited-run drops of hoodies, tees, and accessories from deadstock fabrics and recycled materials.

## Target Audience
18-30 year olds at the intersection of streetwear culture and environmental consciousness. They care about sustainability but would never shop at a hemp store. They want clothes that look like Stussy or Brain Dead, not Patagonia fleece. The sustainability is a value, not an identity.

## Brand Personality
- **Rebellious** — anti-corporate, counter-culture, doesn't play by industry rules
- **Conscious** — aware, intentional, sustainability as quiet conviction not loud virtue
- **Urban** — East London energy, concrete not countryside, night not day
- **Dark** — not goth, but moody. Think streetwear photography at dusk, not sunny lifestyle shoots
- **Anti-corporate** — indie, scrappy, the opposite of greenwashing. Authentic maker energy.

## Competitive Landscape
| Brand | Position | Naming Energy |
|-------|----------|---------------|
| Pangaia | Eco-luxury, material science focus | Greek root, scientific, premium |
| Story MFG | Handmade, artisanal, slow fashion | Descriptive compound, craft narrative |
| 69 Worldwide | Provocative, boundary-pushing | Numeric, confrontational |
| Brain Dead | Counter-culture, art-forward | Dark found words, confrontational |
| Stussy | OG streetwear, cultural credibility | Founder surname |

## Name Requirements
- Works as a streetwear label (on tags, chest embroidery, hat branding)
- Preferably one word or very short
- No corporate compounds (-works, -craft, -hub)
- No obvious eco/green signaling
- Must feel like something you'd see in a streetwear lookbook, not a sustainability report
- Dark/urban register, not pastoral/rural

## Brand Archetypes
**Primary: Outlaw** — Liberation, rebellion, breaking rules
**Secondary: Creator** — Making, expression, craft from nothing

## The Name's #1 Job
Be cool enough to wear. The name should signal insider culture and aesthetic confidence. Sustainability credentials come from the product story, not the brand name.
