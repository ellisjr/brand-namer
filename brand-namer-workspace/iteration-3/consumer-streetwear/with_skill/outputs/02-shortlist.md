## Preference Profile Summary

This brand gravitates toward **dark, monosyllabic found words** that carry industrial or urban weight. The strongest names function through **sensory dissonance** (waste vocabulary for fashion), **dual meaning** (Refuse, Raze, Fray), or **metaphorical precision** (Moth, Crow). The buyer identity projection is **aware rebel who doesn't try hard** — the name should feel like insider vocabulary, not marketing.

The name needs to work at 4 scales:
1. **Whispered:** "Have you heard of [name]?" — works for all 25
2. **Worn:** Embroidered on a chest or printed on a tag — works best for 1-syllable names
3. **Shared:** "@[name] just dropped" — needs to be spellable and distinctive as a handle
4. **Searched:** Someone Googling after hearing the name once — works best for unusual words (Dross, Murk) vs common words (Forge, Flux)
