## Step 01b: Territories & Whitespace

### Competitive Whitespace Mapping

**Competitor naming patterns:**
- **Pangaia** — coined/blended (Pan + Gaia), mythological, earth-mother, Greek roots
- **Story MFG** — descriptive compound, artisanal narrative, abbreviation
- **69 Worldwide** — numeric provocation, genderless, confrontational
- **Brain Dead** — dark compound, subcultural, graphic art, Anglo-Saxon
- **Stussy** — founder surname, cursive logo energy

**Whitespace identified:**
1. Industrial/material vocabulary — no competitor owns this
2. Dark nature / urban ecology — not Pangaia's pristine earth
3. Refusal/dissent language — nobody in sustainable streetwear owns this
4. Found-object / remnant vocabulary — the deadstock concept as territory

### Five Naming Territories

**Territory 1: Salvage Yard**
- Emotional promise: Finding beauty in what was discarded
- Semantic field: deadstock, remnant, offcut, scrap, salvage, reclaim, surplus, residue, cull, shard, relic, slag
- Phonetic profile: Anglo-Saxon, blunt, one-syllable punches, hard consonants
- Anti-patterns: No precious/boutique. No vintage/retro energy.

**Territory 2: Urban Grit**
- Emotional promise: The texture of the city — concrete, soot, industrial East London
- Semantic field: soot, char, ash, flint, slate, grime, grit, smog, rust, oxide, cinder, furnace, kiln, forge
- Phonetic profile: Dark vowels (u, o), hard stops (k, g, d, t), one syllable
- Anti-patterns: Nothing clean or polished. No wellness brand energy.

**Territory 3: Defiance / Refusal**
- Emotional promise: Anti-corporate rebellion — saying no, rejecting the system
- Semantic field: defy, refuse, reject, dissent, void, null, zero, contra, spite, scorn, rogue, feral
- Phonetic profile: Hard attack consonants, short, confrontational, shoutable
- Anti-patterns: Not preachy or activist. Not eco-warrior.

**Territory 4: Dark Nature / Urban Ecology**
- Emotional promise: Nature that survives in hostile environments
- Semantic field: briar, thorn, nettle, dock, moss, lichen, spore, corvid, moth, kestrel, fox, badger, feral, murk, loam, mulch
- Phonetic profile: Mix of soft and hard, dark/earthy vowels
- Anti-patterns: Not Patagonia vibes. Nature that bites back.

**Territory 5: Material / Process**
- Emotional promise: The craft of making — transformation of waste into wearable art
- Semantic field: dye, loom, weft, warp, selvedge, bolt, spool, press, cut, stitch, raw, crude, matte, grain
- Phonetic profile: Short, tactile, consonant-heavy, onomatopoeic
- Anti-patterns: Not luxury atelier. Workshop not showroom.

### Phase 2: Shortlisting Process

#### Evaluation Criteria (Brief-Specific)
1. **Tag test** — Can you see it embroidered on a chest or printed on a woven label?
2. **Badgeability** — Would someone wear a hat that says this?
3. **Bar test** — Can you shout it across a room and be understood?
4. **Streetwear credibility** — Does it belong next to Stussy, Brain Dead, Obey?
5. **Narrative depth** — Does it carry the sustainability/deadstock story without being preachy?
6. **Distinctiveness** — Does it stand apart from Pangaia, Story MFG, 69, Brain Dead, Stussy?

#### SMILE Framework Applied
- **S** (Suggestive): Does it hint at something without over-explaining?
- **M** (Memorable): Will someone remember it after hearing it once?
- **I** (Imagery): Does it evoke a mental picture?
- **L** (Legs): Can it extend to drops, collabs, sub-lines?
- **E** (Emotional): Does it trigger the right feeling — dark, rebellious, conscious?

### Definitions for Obscure Picks

**Dross** (n.) — "scum or unwanted material formed on the surface of molten metal; worthless or trivial matter." From Old English "drōs" (dregs, dirt). As a brand: "What industry calls dross, we call material."

**Sumi** (n.) — Japanese: "ink" or "charcoal" (墨). Made by burning pine or lamp oil and collecting the soot, then mixing with animal glue. As a brand: the connection between soot, charcoal, ink, and creation is native to the word.

**Rue** (n./v.) — "A perennial herb (Ruta graveolens) known for its bitter taste and medicinal use; to feel regret." Also: French for "street" (la rue). From Latin "ruta." As a brand: triple meaning — the herb, the feeling, the street.

**Stave** (n./v.) — "A narrow length of wood forming part of a barrel or boat; to avert or delay something." From Old English "stæf." As a brand: building something new from individual pieces, staving off destruction.

**Fray** (n./v.) — "A battle or brawl; to unravel or become worn at the edge." From Latin "fricare" (to rub). As a brand: entering the fray (fight) while fraying the edges of fashion convention.
