# Brand Naming Session: AI-Powered Legal Document Review Platform

**Date:** 2025-03-25
**Brief:** AI-powered contract analysis, risk flagging, term extraction, and playbook comparison for legal ops teams and in-house counsel (10+ lawyer organizations). Seed stage, $4M raised. Must feel authoritative and precise without being stuffy Latin. Regulated industry constraints apply. International from day one (UK/EU).

**Competitive landscape:** Kira Systems, eBrevia, Luminance, Ironclad, ContractPodAi, Harvey
**Tone targets:** Authoritative, precise, modern, built-for-lawyers-but-not-a-law-firm. "Clio/Relativity energy."
**Constraints:** No 'attorney', 'counsel', 'legal advice'. No played-out Latin (Lexicon, Juris, Veritas). Must work internationally.

## Observations on Competitive Whitespace

Looking at the competitor set — Kira, eBrevia, Luminance, Ironclad, ContractPodAi, Harvey:

- **Kira, Harvey** = human names (approachable, but the space may be getting crowded here)
- **Luminance** = light metaphor (elegant, scientific)
- **Ironclad** = strength metaphor (bold, protective)
- **eBrevia, ContractPodAi** = descriptive/functional (dated naming style)

**Whitespace opportunities:**
1. **Legal-native vocabulary** (Proviso, Precept, Construe, Covenant) — none of the competitors do this
2. **Precision instruments** (Parse, Caliber, Meridian) — Luminance touches this but from the light angle only
3. **Mythological authority** (Argus, Aegis, Themis) — no competitor occupies this space
4. **Nature/material** (Obsidian, Cairn, Flint) — completely unoccupied territory in legaltech
5. **British heritage names** (Ashford, Calder, Pemberton) — would signal UK market nativity strongly

The safest competitive positioning would be either the "legal-native vocabulary" lane (Proviso, Precept) or the "precision action" lane (Parse, Sift, Distill) — both are unoccupied and deeply aligned with the product's value proposition.
