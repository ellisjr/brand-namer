### 5 Surviving Territories

**1. Precision Instrument**
- Emotional promise: Exactness, calibration, scientific rigor applied to legal language
- Semantic field: caliber, gauge, benchmark, index, meridian, plumb, level, assay, proof, standard, measure, grade, tolerance, resolution
- Phonetic profile: Clean consonants, high/front vowels (i, e) for precision. Trochaic stress. 2-3 syllables.
- Anti-patterns: No soft/warm sounds. No playful energy.

**2. Sentinel / Oversight**
- Emotional promise: Vigilance, governance, trustworthy watchkeeping
- Semantic field: sentinel, watch, vigil, warden, steward, keeper, post, garrison, ward, sentry, patrol, muster, standing
- Phonetic profile: Institutional weight. Hard consonants for authority. Nasal warmth for trust. Anglo-Saxon roots.
- Anti-patterns: No military aggression. No Shield/Protect/Defend over-claiming.

**3. Optics / Clarity**
- Emotional promise: Seeing what others miss -- revealing hidden risk, making the complex transparent
- Semantic field: lens, scope, prism, aperture, focus, resolve, refract, spectrum, parallax, acuity, lumen, lucid, pellucid, parse
- Phonetic profile: Fricatives and liquids for flow and clarity. Open vowels. Clean, bright feel.
- Anti-patterns: No overlap with Luminance. No vague 'vision/insight' corporate-speak.

**4. Signal / Pattern**
- Emotional promise: Intelligence gathering, pattern detection, finding the anomaly in the noise
- Semantic field: signal, cipher, glyph, rune, parse, flag, trace, marker, beacon, vector, delta, threshold, triage, query
- Phonetic profile: Short, punchy. Plosives for precision. Staccato rhythm. Technical credibility.
- Anti-patterns: No consumer-app energy. Must feel enterprise-grade.

**5. Domain Figures (Personified)**
- Emotional promise: Authority of a name -- mythological, historical, literary figures of justice/wisdom/discernment
- Semantic field: Themis, Athena, Solon, Portia, Cicero, Blackstone, Littleton, Astraea, Maat, Dike, Eunomia
- Phonetic profile: Classical weight. 2-3 syllables. Must pass "coffee shop test."
- Anti-patterns: No obvious/overused mythology. No names requiring explanation.
