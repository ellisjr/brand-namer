### Competitive Whitespace Mapping

|---|---|---|
| Yellowbird | Color + Animal -- warm, nature | Compound (nature) |
| Secret Aardvark | Adjective + Arbitrary Animal -- quirky | Compound (arbitrary) |

**Overused in broader hot sauce category:** fire, flame, burn, hell, devil, death, skull, ghost, atomic, inferno, dragon

**Whitespace identified:**
1. Process/Craft -- nobody names around the making (fermentation, patience)
2. Earth/Terroir -- no one owns the "soil, land, growing" space
3. Sensory texture beyond heat -- flavor complexity unoccupied
4. Irreverent/absurd -- broader absurdist naming (Liquid Death energy) wide open

### Territories Explored and Dropped
- "Volcanic/Geological" -- dropped: overlaps with fire/heat cliches
- "Pain/Endurance" -- dropped: default hot sauce territory
- "Laboratory/Science" -- dropped: clashes with craft/authentic personality
- "Heritage/Place" -- dropped: Cholula and Fly By Jing already own cultural specificity

### 5 Final Territories

**1. Slow Alchemy**
- Emotional promise: Patience, transformation, craft mastery
- Semantic field: ferment, culture, slow, steep, age, cure, brine, barrel, time, turn, ripen, bloom, mother
- Phonetic profile: Liquid consonants (l, r), open vowels, flowing rhythm
- Anti-patterns: No clinical, industrial, or rushed sounds

**2. Dark Earth**
- Emotional promise: Grounded, primal, connected to soil and land
- Semantic field: loam, root, furrow, clay, peat, silt, mulch, grove, understory, mycelium, humus
- Phonetic profile: Anglo-Saxon roots, hard consonants with nasal warmth, back vowels
- Anti-patterns: No precious, boutique, or suburban-farm-cute

**3. Animal / Nature Curator** (default check for consumer brief)
- Emotional promise: Instinctive, alive, creature-energy
- Semantic field: crow, magpie, badger, mole, wasp, kestrel, newt, salamander, viper, mantis
- Phonetic profile: Short, punchy, 1-2 syllables, hard-stop consonants
- Anti-patterns: No overlap with Yellowbird or Secret Aardvark. No cute.

**4. Brazen Irreverence**
- Emotional promise: Swagger, confidence, a wink
- Semantic field: gall, nerve, audacity, cheek, brass, mouth, tongue, gut, grit, moxie, feral
- Phonetic profile: Plosives (b, k, p, t), short syllables, staccato rhythm
- Anti-patterns: No juvenile, fratty, or try-hard

**5. Smoke & Char**
- Emotional promise: Warmth, depth, complexity
- Semantic field: ember, char, cinder, smolder, ash, soot, kiln, hearth, flint, scorch
- Phonetic profile: Fricatives (sh, ch) and sibilants mixed with hard stops
- Anti-patterns: No fire/flame cliches. Warmth/depth not destruction/pain.

### Definitions for Obscure/Foreign Shortlisted Names

**Tilth** (n.) -- "The state of being tilled; cultivated soil ready for planting." From Old English "tilth" (cultivation, husbandry). As a brand: the soil that grew the peppers, the care in preparation.

**Ferox** (adj., Latin) -- "Fierce, wild, bold, spirited." From Latin "ferus" (wild) + "-ox" intensifier. Used in species names (Salmo ferox = fierce trout). As a brand: fierce authenticity.

**Koji** (n.) -- "Aspergillus oryzae, a fermentation mold essential to miso, soy sauce, sake, and other East Asian fermented foods." From Japanese. As a brand: the invisible organism that transforms ingredients through patience.

**Gall** (n.) -- "1. Bold impudence, nerve. 2. Bile, a bitter fluid. 3. A plant growth caused by parasites." From Old English "gealla" (bile). As a brand: the audacity to make something this bold.

**Loam** (n.) -- "Fertile soil composed of clay, silt, and sand in balanced proportions; the ideal growing medium." From Old English "lam" (clay, earth). As a brand: the perfect earth that grows perfect peppers.

**Crock** (n.) -- "An earthenware pot or jar used for fermentation and food storage." From Old English "crocc" (pot). As a brand: the vessel where transformation happens.
