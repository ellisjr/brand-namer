# Brand Naming Session: Premium Men's Skincare Line

## Brief Summary

**Target:** Men aged 28-45, style-conscious, GQ-reader archetype, willing to invest in quality skincare but averse to overly feminine or clinical branding. DTC-first, Nordstrom/Saks trajectory.

**Brand Feel:** Elevated but masculine, clinical-meets-luxury, no-nonsense, confident.

**Benchmark:** Aesop meets The Ordinary, but built specifically for men.

**Product Line:** Cleanser, moisturizer, eye cream, SPF.

**Visual Test:** Must look good on a matte black bottle.

**Competitive Set:** Lumin, Kiehl's, Bulldog, Harry's, Hims.

## NAMING OBSERVATIONS & STRATEGIC NOTES

### What to avoid (based on competitive analysis)
- **Lumin** owns the light/luminosity space — avoid glow/radiance/light names
- **Bulldog** owns the playful-masculine space — avoid animal names
- **Hims** owns the casual-abbreviation space — avoid "his/him" derivatives
- **Harry's** owns the everyman-heritage space — avoid first-name brands
- **Kiehl's** owns the apothecary-heritage space — avoid "since 18XX" naming conventions

### Strongest naming territories for this brief
1. **Geological/Material** (Basalt, Cairn, Stratum, Slate, Flint) — untapped in men's skincare, naturally masculine, no gender baggage
2. **Craft/Workshop** (Hone, Temper, Alloy, Proof) — speaks to the "quality goods" mindset of the GQ reader
3. **Japanese Minimalism** (Kanso, Sumi, Shibui) — signals design sophistication, appeals to the "nice watch" guy

### Domain & trademark considerations
- Four-letter names (Hone, Tarn, Helm, Vero) are harder to get as .com but excellent for brand recall
- Consider .co, get[name].com, or [name]skin.com as alternatives
- Geological terms (Basalt, Cairn, Stratum) likely have cleaner trademark paths in skincare (Class 3)
- Japanese terms (Kanso, Sumi) offer strong international trademark potential

### The matte-black-bottle test
Names that would look best in a minimal sans-serif typeface on matte black packaging:
- **CAIRN** — strong, balanced letterforms
- **HONE** — four even letters, satisfying symmetry
- **BASALT** — the word itself looks like dark stone
- **VERO** — clean, open, European
- **HELM** — compact, authoritative
- **SUMI** — minimal, artful
